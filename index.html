About Us

British Videogame Institute has been established to address the serious skills shortage in the videogames industry. The courses from the British Videogame Institute are designed with the help of actual videogame industry professionals. All the courses are intended to give the students a comprehensive foundation in the practical skills required to produce next generation videogames.
To learn more, Contact Us.

Videogame Project
A unique feature of the British Videogame Institute courses is the Final Videogame Project. Unlike a generic, theoretical project offered by traditional universities and institutes, our final project is part of an actual videogame that is already in production. This means that all students will get hands on experience by working on a videogame that will be shipping worldwide. 
The exact details about the type of videogame in the final project will be provided directly to the students during the course. The students are welcome to use a demo from the videogame as part of their portfolio while applying for jobs after graduation.

FAQ

 What kind of equipment will the students have?

All students will have access to a dedicated PC for the duration of the course. The PC will be given pre-installed with all the required software for the course. They will also have broadband internet access.

Students can also make use of the game consoles available at the institute. The collection includes Sony PlayStation 2, 3 and 4, Microsoft Xbox One and Nintendo Switch. There is also a library of hundreds of PC and console games to which new titles are added on a monthly basis. Several TVs are also available to connect to the consoles. 

 What is the current employment scenario for junior programmers and artists in the games industry?

The videogames industry is now bigger than box office Hollywood and still growing. Recently, the industry has expanded into new areas like VR, AR and smart TVs. Therefore, the employment situation has never been better for a junior programmer or artist looking to break into the industry.

However, the side effect of this rapid expansion has been a high degree of specialisation. It is no longer sufficient to have an interest in games and promote yourself as a generic programmer or artist. While applying for a job in the industry, even a junior programmer or artist needs to show a great level of specialisation. The British Videogame Institute courses have been created with exactly these considerations in mind. 

 Can I complete my final videogame project in Britain?

Yes. You can complete your final project at our HQ in Cambridge or at one of our regional centers around the world.

Do you arrange accommodation for foreign students in Britain?

Yes.

 Do you have an employment program for new graduates?

Yes. It is our earnest desire to see every one of our students working in the videogame industry. Towards this end, we have created an active Employment Cell which maintains constant relations with potential employers in the UK and all the countries that we operate in.

To ensure maximum success, we do not blindly submit applications on behalf of our students to all available positions. As far as possible, we try to make direct contact with the employer and then introduce only the students most qualified for the positions available
